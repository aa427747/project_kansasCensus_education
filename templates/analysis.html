Foundation Goal

    Determine if there is a positive correlation between higher earnings and higher levels of education.

  • Is there a financial benefit of pursuing higher level education?
  • How does the county size impact median earnings?
  • What is the wage gap between men and women based on different factors?
  • What is the education level by race?

Data Sources

United State Census American Fact Results

May 2019 Analysis

    Data findings:
  • Men out earn women in every country in Kansas.
  • On average, in Kansas, women make about $26,000 median income and men make $40,000 median income- for every $1 a man makes, a woman makes $0.65.
  • In Johnson County, the median income wage gap is almost $21,000.
  • There are more high school graduates per race than bachelors degree.
  • Johnson county shows a high level of high school graduation.
  • In Johnson county, 56% of the "white" population has a bachelors degree.
  • Comanche county (pop 1790), the wage gap is much closer, not every race is represented in the county, the level of HS is fairly high (94% of whites have a HS diploma but only 19% have a bachelors) but bachelors is very low.
  • The circle layer better denotes the population differences within the smaller and larger eastern counties.
  • Median income does show to be similar, but there are some counties showing as undefined, which may be creating irregularities in the map.
  • In the March analysis, we saw larger low income in the higher populated counties, which is why we can see the median income level out.
  • Eastern counties have higher Bachelors and Grads income counts than the western counties. There is a difference between them. Thomas to Johnson county has a $20-30k difference in income.
  • We were not able to pull the Choropleth map because we didn't have Geojson data.
